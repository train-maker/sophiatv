04 — Frequently asked

What founders ask before they sign.

Why only 100 seats?

Restraint. A larger pool dilutes the dividend, cheapens the badge, and crowds the founder calls. One hundred is enough to fund the next stage of building Sophia and small enough that every seat-holder is known by name.

Is this an investment? Equity?

Treat it as a premium lifetime membership with a contractual profit-share rider. We are not selling shares of the operating company. The dividend is a contractual obligation, not equity, and it does not grant a vote on share-class matters or M&A. Final structure will be documented by securities counsel before any payment is accepted, and may be issued under an applicable exemption (Reg D 506(c) for accredited investors, Reg CF, or a similar pathway). We will tell every applicant which exemption applies before they wire funds.

What if Sophia is acquired or goes public?

Founder seats survive the corporate event. The buyer or successor entity assumes the obligations under the Founder Agreement: lifetime access, annual dividend pool, badge, voting on community decisions. The acquirer is bound to the same five-percent-of-net-profit rate.

What if Sophia never turns a profit?

The dividend is zero in unprofitable years. The rest of the membership — lifetime access, badge, voting, concierge — remains in force as long as Sophia operates. Sophia's path to profitability is a Featured tier at $29.99/mo with a current run-rate already in the operator base.

Can I transfer the seat?

Yes, after year five. Earlier transfers require Sophia's approval and are typically reserved for cases of estate planning or operator retirement. Transferred seats retain the badge and the dividend pool position; the new founder takes over the rights.

How do I know my dividend is calculated honestly?

Annual financials are reviewed by an independent accounting firm. The Founder Agreement requires a copy of the audited statement to be delivered to every founder before the dividend pool is set. Founders also have a contractual right to request an additional review, paid for collectively by the circle, once every three years.

What does $10,000 actually buy at retail?

Featured listing on SophiaMarket is $29.99/mo ($359.88/yr). Enterprise tier is $299/mo ($3,588/yr). Across a 30-year operating horizon, the retail value of a single Enterprise lifetime is north of $100,000 — before counting the dividend, voting, and onboarding. The $10,000 entry is a deliberate discount in exchange for being among the first hundred and being public about it.

How long is the offering open?

Until 100 seats are claimed, or until 31 December 2026, whichever comes first. After the cap, a waitlist opens at higher pricing for any seats released by transfer.
