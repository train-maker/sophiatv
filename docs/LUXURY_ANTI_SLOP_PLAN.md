# Sophia Luxury Anti-Slop Plan

## Goal
Remove every visible sign of generic AI website output and bring Sophia into a high-end, luxury, futuristic product standard across the full site.

Sophia's larger product mission is to bring the world together in more ways than one: language, trade, culture, creators, small businesses, learning, and everyday human connection.

## Million-Dollar Quality Bar
Sophia should be built as if investors, enterprise buyers, creators, and global operators are judging whether this is a serious product company. The target is not "nice landing page." The target is a credible premium web app and brand system that could justify a seven-figure valuation story through execution quality, product clarity, trust, and operational readiness.

A million-dollar Sophia surface must show:

- a clear product promise in the first 5 seconds
- a premium visual identity that is consistent across every page
- working core flows, not decorative demos
- honest launch-state messaging for anything still in preview
- real account/backend readiness for the free-period launch
- social and marketplace surfaces that feel usable, not conceptual
- strong trust language around payments, privacy, moderation, and account data
- responsive layouts that feel designed for mobile, not squeezed down
- no visible placeholders, fake telemetry, or generic generated copy
- verification evidence before anything is called done

## World-Connection Product Principles
1. Translation is not just words; it is access to people, markets, cultures, and opportunity.
2. Marketplace listings should help real buyers and sellers find each other across borders.
3. Social posts should create useful signals, not noisy feeds.
4. City/state/country pages should feel like practical local gateways, not SEO filler.
5. Business directory pages should make small businesses look legitimate and reachable.
6. Trust tools matter: reporting, moderation, verified contact paths, and clear listing rules.
7. Cultural design should feel global without borrowing sacred or stereotyped imagery.
8. Every user path should answer: who can I meet, what can I understand, what can I build, what can I buy or sell?

## Claude Cowork Critique
Claude's staff-engineer/product critique: the current visual bones are promising, but premium animation and glass styling are carrying too much of the perceived quality. The gap to a million-dollar product is the proof layer:

- live indicators must be real or removed
- backend-backed social activity must replace local-only demo behavior
- payment language must match the 30-day free launch state
- trust signals must be concrete, not decorative
- performance must hold up on real mobile
- every page needs consistent product credibility, not just atmosphere

Highest-leverage move: remove fake/missing live indicators and ship one real end-to-end account/social flow first. Stripe checkout stays deferred until paid billing starts after the free launch period.

## Design Standard
Sophia should feel like quiet luxury plus futuristic precision:

- black, gold, glass, metallic, and disciplined neutral contrast
- real product value before decoration
- premium logo treatment on every major screen
- centered black/gold globe only where it supports the page
- clear first viewport on mobile and desktop
- no purple-on-black text
- no clipped logos, badges, headings, or buttons
- no fake live metrics, fake testimonials, or filler hype
- no decorative clutter that looks generated or cheap

## Visual North Star
Sharp around the edges, shining like diamonds, beautiful as a flower in light summer rain.

Interpretation for implementation:

- sharp edges: precise grids, aligned logos, crisp cards, no sloppy crop or loose spacing
- diamond light: controlled highlights, metallic gold reflection, small glints only where they guide attention
- flower softness: humane copy, generous breathing room, graceful curves in hero imagery and transitions
- summer rain: subtle glass, clean depth, calm motion, atmospheric texture without haze or clutter
- luxury restraint: one memorable visual moment per page, not ten competing effects

Extended art direction:

- rainbow after rain: restrained spectral color only as a rare highlight, never a loud gradient wash
- clear blue ocean: depth, clarity, calm motion, and breathable negative space
- dolphin arc: graceful transitions that feel alive but controlled
- polished wooden floor: warm gloss, craftsmanship, reflection, and surfaces worth staring at
- stare-worthy finish: every first viewport should reward a second look without confusing the user
- statues of Rome: timeless proportion, sculpted hierarchy, human elegance, and confident restraint
- pyramids of Egypt: monumental geometry, symbolic clarity, durability, and awe without clutter
- hidden rainforest caves: depth, mystery, discovery, organic shadow, and mesmerizing reveal without visual clutter

## Anti-Slop Rules
1. No fake data presented as live activity.
2. No decorative badge, chip, glow, or panel unless it communicates a real state.
3. No purple/pink-on-black legacy styling.
4. No clipped logo, globe, text descender, card title, or button label.
5. No generic phrases like "unlock your potential" unless tied to a concrete feature.
6. No CTA that routes to a dead, misleading, or backend-missing flow.
7. No page should look like a different brand from the home page.
8. No backend-preview feature should pretend to be production-ready.
9. No payment language should imply live billing during the 30-day free period.
10. No page passes without desktop and mobile browser verification.
11. No cartoons, mascot art, toy-like illustrations, or childish visual metaphors.
12. No simple filler shapes. Geometry must feel architectural, sculptural, precious, and intentional.
13. No generic AI-looking glass blobs, orbs, random gradients, or decorative noise.

## Geometry Standard
Every shape on Sophia must earn its place.

- Use monumental geometry: circles, arcs, pillars, rings, horizons, pyramidal balance, and precise grids.
- Let geometry frame real product meaning: globe, marketplace pathways, translation bridges, trust anchors, and social signal flow.
- Prefer sculpted edges, polished surfaces, fine lines, restrained reflections, and precise alignment.
- Avoid random floating shapes, cartoon icons, stock vectors, and simple decorative doodles.
- If a shape is present, it should look like it was carved, cast, polished, or engineered.

## Depth And Discovery
Sophia should reward exploration like a hidden cave in the heart of a rainforest:

- layered entrances: each section should invite one clear next step
- shadow with purpose: dark areas create focus, not murkiness
- light reveals: gold, crystal, or ocean highlights should uncover content
- organic depth: combine natural atmosphere with engineered layout
- no maze: mystery should be beautiful, but navigation must stay obvious

## Page Audit Checklist
For each page, inspect and fix:

- first viewport brand signal
- logo size, crop, and alignment
- headline line height and descenders
- CTA clarity and route validity
- mobile nav fit
- text contrast and color palette
- spacing rhythm
- card nesting and over-decoration
- empty/loading/error states
- real backend state versus preview/local copy
- console errors
- horizontal overflow
- accessibility landmarks and labels

## Visual System Fixes
1. Lock one premium typography scale.
2. Standardize buttons: gold primary, glass secondary, quiet text links.
3. Standardize cards: max 8px radius unless tool-specific, no card-inside-card.
4. Replace leftover purple/pink styles with gold, silver, white, or neutral glass.
5. Tighten all hero sections so the next section is hinted on desktop and mobile.
6. Replace decorative badges with real product status only.
7. Make all form states feel premium: success, error, disabled, loading.

## Backend-Free Launch Alignment
During the 30-day free launch period:

- Supabase auth and backend credentials are active.
- Stripe secret/webhook variables are present.
- Stripe price IDs are intentionally deferred until paid billing starts.
- Payment buttons must not imply immediate paid checkout if checkout is not active.
- Pricing copy must clearly say 30-day free launch access.

## Definition Of Done
A page is done only when:

- it looks premium on mobile and desktop
- the first viewport is not clipped
- no visible AI-slop decorations remain
- forms and buttons either work or state their preview status honestly
- Playwright shows no blocking console/page errors
- text is readable and not purple-on-black
- navigation reaches the expected pages
- `npm run check` and Playwright pass after the full pass
