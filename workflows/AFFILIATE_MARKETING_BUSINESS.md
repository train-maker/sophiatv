# Affiliate Marketing Business Workflow

Last checked: 2026-04-25

Purpose: build a legitimate affiliate marketing lane around useful tools, honest recommendations, and search/social content that helps people solve everyday problems.

## Business Position

Sophia affiliate content should bring people useful options, not manipulate them into clicks.

Core angle:

- simple apps,
- everyday tools,
- travel and packing,
- boating and summer gear,
- creator/business tools,
- home organization,
- natural wellness education with careful claims,
- language/travel/global market tools.

Avoid:

- fake reviews,
- copied product descriptions,
- AI-generated mass pages with no original value,
- undisclosed affiliate links,
- health cure claims,
- spammy comments, DMs, or email blasts,
- pretending to own or officially represent another brand.

## Compliance Rules

### FTC

Disclose affiliate relationships clearly and close to the recommendation or link.

Default disclosure:

```text
Disclosure: This page may contain affiliate links. If you buy through them, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.
```

Rules:

- Do not hide disclosures in footer-only text.
- Do not bury disclosure in a pile of hashtags.
- Do not recommend products we have no basis to discuss.
- Do not claim results we cannot prove.
- For video/audio, disclose in the content itself and in the description.

### Google Search

Build people-first content, not search-engine-first pages.

Every affiliate page must add original value:

- comparison criteria,
- who it is for / not for,
- limitations,
- setup steps,
- buyer checklist,
- screenshots or original examples where possible,
- safer alternatives or low-cost options.

Do not publish thin affiliate pages that only restate merchant info.

### Link Handling

Use appropriate link attributes on affiliate links:

```html
rel="sponsored nofollow"
```

For Amazon Associates or other programs, follow each program's exact disclosure and link-format rules.

## Operating Workflow

1. Pick one niche problem.
2. Find products/services that honestly fit the problem.
3. Build a helpful page before adding affiliate links.
4. Add clear disclosure near the first recommendation.
5. Add comparison table, buyer checklist, and "best for / avoid if" notes.
6. Verify links, metadata, mobile layout, and disclosure visibility.
7. Publish only after Tarence approves accounts, affiliate programs, and any public deployment.

## Approval Gates

Stop for Tarence before:

- creating affiliate accounts,
- entering tax/payment details,
- using business Gmail/account permissions,
- adding tracking pixels,
- buying domains,
- running paid ads,
- publishing public pages under Tarence's identity,
- making health/legal/financial claims,
- emailing or DMing people.
